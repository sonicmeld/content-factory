# CONTENT_MODEL.md

## Purpose

This document defines the core business entities of Content Factory.

It serves as the source of truth for understanding how content flows through the platform.

---

# Core Principle

Content Factory is NOT an AI content generation platform.

Content Factory consumes assets and metadata generated by external systems and manages:

* Channel Operations
* Asset Organization
* Content Packaging
* Upload Scheduling
* YouTube Distribution

# Primary Business Entity

## Content Package

A Content Package is the primary business entity of Content Factory.

A Content Package represents a complete publishable video unit.

Example:

Sleep Sounds #001

Contains:

* video.mp4
* timestamp.txt

A Content Package is assigned to:

* One Channel
* One Upload Schedule
* One Upload Queue Entry

A Content Package is the smallest publishable unit inside the platform.

---

# Supporting Entities

## Channel

Represents a YouTube channel.

Contains:

* Channel Name
* Slug
* OAuth Credentials
* Upload Preferences
* Publishing Configuration

A Channel owns:

* Content Packages
* Upload Jobs
* Channel Assets

---

## Upload Job

Represents a scheduled publishing task.

Contains:

* Content Package
* Target Channel
* Schedule
* Upload Status

Status Examples:

* Pending
* Scheduled
* Uploading
* Published
* Failed

---

# Asset Library

Asset Library stores reusable production assets.

Assets are used during content production.

Assets are NOT publishable entities.

Examples:

* Footage Images
* Audio Assets
* Prompt Packs
* Reference Images
* Thumbnail References

Asset Library supports content production but is not the primary business entity.

---

# Asset Scope

Assets can exist in two scopes.

## Shared Asset

Available across multiple channels.

Examples:

* Generic Footage
* Ambient Audio
* Prompt Templates
* Reference Images

Shared Assets are reusable platform-wide.

---

## Channel Asset

Available only to a specific channel.

Examples:

* Channel Branding
* Niche-specific Footage
* Custom References
* Channel Prompt Packs

Channel Assets help maintain channel identity.

---

# Production Assets

## Footage

Footage is a production asset.

Footage is one of the most important assets in the system.

Examples:

* AI Generated Images
* Scene Collections
* Visual Sequences

Footage is combined with audio during video production to create:

* video.mp4

Footage can be:

* Shared
* Channel Specific

---

## Audio

Audio is a production asset.

Examples:

* Ambient Sounds
* Sleep Sounds
* Music Loops
* Narration

Audio is combined with footage during video production.

---

# Publishing Metadata

Publishing metadata is not considered a Content Package asset.

Publishing metadata may be generated by AI during the publishing workflow.

Examples:

* Title
* Description
* Thumbnail

Metadata generation may be provided by:

* 9Router AI Infrastructure

Content Factory consumes the results.

---

# Channel Workspace

Each channel operates within its own workspace.

Example:

Midnight Cassette Workspace

* Overview
* Prompt Factory
* Assets
* Content Packages
* Upload Queue
* Published Videos
* Settings

The workspace is the operational center of a channel.

---

# External Systems

## 9Router AI Infrastructure

9Router is an external production system.

Provides:

* Research Combos
* AI Skills
* Footage Generation
* Metadata Generation
* Provider Routing
* Account Rotation
* Quota Distribution

Produces:

* Footage Images
* Titles
* Descriptions
* Thumbnails

Content Factory consumes these outputs.

Content Factory does not generate them.
